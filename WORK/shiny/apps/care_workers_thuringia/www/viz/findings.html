📋 Executive Summary

This research mapped the landscape of healthcare organizations involved in the recruitment journey of care workers in Thuringia. Through systematic data collection from two primary sources—Weltoffenes Thüringen and the Federal Employment Agency (Bundesagentur für Arbeit)—we identified 59 relevant organizations operating across the region.

Key Insight

The care sector in Thuringia is dominated by health and social work organizations (83%), with a strong concentration in urban centers, particularly Erfurt (30.5% of all organizations).

🏛️ Service Types

Breakdown by Service Category

Within the health and social work sector, organizations provide diverse services:

  • Outpatient Nursing Care (Home Care): 9 organizations providing ambulatory care services directly to patients' homes
  • Residential Care for Elderly: 9 organizations operating nursing homes and assisted living facilities
  • General Hospitals: 7 organizations including university clinics and regional hospitals
  • Social Counseling Services: 5 organizations offering guidance and support services
  • Ambulance Services: 2 organizations (DRK) providing emergency medical services
  • Vocational Training: 3 organizations specializing in healthcare education

📍 Geographic Distribution

Concentration in Urban Centers

Organizations are unevenly distributed across Thuringia, with significant concentration in major cities:

Erfurt (18 orgs - 30.5%) Weimar (7 orgs - 11.9%) Jena (6 orgs - 10.2%) Gera (3 orgs - 5.1%) Rudolstadt (3 orgs - 5.1%) Hildburghausen (2 orgs) Nordhausen (2 orgs) Meiningen (2 orgs) Bad Salzungen (2 orgs)

Urban-Rural Gap

Over 50% of identified organizations are located in just three cities (Erfurt, Weimar, Jena), highlighting a potential gap in care infrastructure in rural areas of Thuringia.

🏢 Key Stakeholders

Major Welfare Organizations

Several large welfare associations play a central role in the care landscape:

  • Arbeiterwohlfahrt (AWO): Multiple regional branches providing diverse social services
  • Deutsches Rotes Kreuz (DRK): Emergency services and social care across Thuringia
  • Diakonie: Protestant welfare organization with numerous care facilities
  • Caritas: Catholic welfare services including elderly care
  • Johanniter: Emergency services and senior care facilities
  • LIGA der Freien Wohlfahrtspflege: Umbrella organization coordinating welfare providers

Migration-Focused Organizations

Several organizations specifically address migration and integration:

  • MigraNetz Thüringen e.V.: Network supporting migrant communities
  • DaMigra e.V.: Organization for migrant women
  • DGB Hessen-Thüringen: Trade union supporting worker rights including migrants

💡 Implications for Care Worker Recruitment

Opportunities

  • Strong network of established welfare organizations provides potential entry points for international care workers
  • Presence of vocational training institutions enables qualification recognition and further education
  • Migration-focused organizations can serve as bridges for integration support
  • Diverse service types offer varied career paths within the care sector

Challenges

  • Geographic concentration in urban areas may limit opportunities in rural regions
  • Limited number of staffing agencies (3) may indicate reliance on direct hiring
  • Need for coordination between healthcare providers and migration support organizations
  • Potential language and cultural barriers requiring targeted support programs

📐 Methodology

📅 Data Collection Period

November 2025 — Data was collected through systematic searches of two primary sources.

🌐 Source 1: Weltoffenes Thüringen

Democracy-promoting initiative with 8,000+ members. Organizations filtered using healthcare and migration-related keywords.

💼 Source 2: Bundesagentur für Arbeit

Federal Employment Agency job portal. Search for "Pflegefachkraft" positions within 100km of Erfurt.

📊 Classification System

Organizations classified using WZ 2008 (Klassifikation der Wirtschaftszweige) — the German standard for economic activity classification.
